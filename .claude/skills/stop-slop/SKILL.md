## What to remove

- Hedge padding: "it's worth noting", "it is important to note that", "in general", "arguably".
- Empty transitions: "furthermore", "moreover", "additionally" used to link unrelated sentences rather than because the logic requires it.
- Inflated adjectives with no supporting evidence: "robust", "powerful", "comprehensive", "novel", "cutting-edge".
- Rule-of-three padding ("fast, flexible, and scalable") when one or two of the three aren't actually demonstrated.
- Throat-clearing openers ("In this section, we will discuss...", "This paper presents...") — cut straight to the claim.
- Repetition of the same point across consecutive sentences in different words.
- Passive voice used to dodge specifying who/what did something, when the actor is known.

## What to keep

- Precise claims with a number, citation, or table/figure reference attached.
- Technical terms the paper already defines.
- Section transitions that carry actual logical weight (e.g., "because X, we do Y").

## Process

1. Read the target section in full first — don't edit sentence-by-sentence blind.
2. Cut filler and hedges outright rather than rewording them.
3. Where a claim lacks support, flag it back to the user instead of quietly softening or removing the claim.
4. Keep sentence length varied; don't over-correct into staccato fragments.
